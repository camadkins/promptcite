## Why dual-license

PromptCite is open-source infrastructure for AI-use disclosure in education.
The AGPL v3 community license keeps the code free for the students,
researchers, and contributors the project exists to serve. The commercial
license funds maintenance and prevents quiet SaaS capture — anyone wrapping
PromptCite in a hosted service either open-sources their modifications under
AGPL or pays for a commercial license.

This is the same pattern used by MongoDB, Grafana, Metabase, and Plausible.
It is well-established and OSI-compatible.

## The AGPL v3 "network use is distribution" clause

AGPL v3 differs from GPL in one important way: if you run a modified version
of PromptCite as a network service that users interact with, you must offer
those users the modified source code. This prevents the "private SaaS fork"
loophole where a vendor would otherwise improve the codebase and never
contribute back. If this clause is incompatible with your deployment, that's
exactly when the commercial license applies.

## Contribution

All contributions to PromptCite require a signed Contributor License
Agreement (CLA). See `CLA.md` for the agreement and the project's CLA bot
(cla-assistant.io) for the automated signing flow.

The CLA grants the maintainer the right to relicense your contribution under
commercial terms while preserving your right to use your own contribution
under AGPL v3. This is what makes dual-licensing legally durable as the
project grows.
